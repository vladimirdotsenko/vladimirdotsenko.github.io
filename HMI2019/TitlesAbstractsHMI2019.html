Mini-courses:

Bernhard Keller (Paris 7)
Title: Hochschild (co-)homology and differential graded categories

Abstract: We will report on functoriality and invariance results for the Hochschild (co-)homology of algebras, categories and differential graded categories. These will include functoriality of Hochschild homology (more precisely: the mixed complex) with respect to derived functors preserving perfection, and functoriality of Hochschild cohomology (more precisely: the B-infinity algebra computing it) with respect to fully faithful derived functors. Towards the end of the minicourse, we will concentrate on Hochschild cohomology. Highlights will be Lowen-Van den Bergh's derived version of the isomorphism between the center of an algebra and its module category as well as a recent analogous statement for Tate-Hochschild cohomology, with applications to the reconstruction of isolated singularities.

Notes of the mini-course are available on the webpage of Bernhard Keller here.


Sarah Witherspoon (Texas A&M)
Title: Hochschild cohomology of algebras

Abstract: Hochschild cohomology is an important tool for understanding the structure and representations of associative algebras. The Hochschild cohomology of an algebra is itself an associative algebra (under cup product) and a graded Lie algebra (under Gerstenhaber bracket). These two binary operations on Hochschild cohomology are essential for some of its applications.

This series of lectures will examine in detail the algebraic structure on Hochschild cohomology provided by the cup product and Gerstenhaber bracket. We will begin with an introduction to Hochschild cohomology and these two operations. We will then specialize to algebras over a field, in which case the Hochschild cohomology algebra can be defined via Ext, leading to many equivalent ways to view the cup product and Gerstenhaber bracket. We will focus particularly on recent results on brackets, including Volkov's homotopy lifting method for defining the bracket by way of an arbitrary resolution, Suarez-Alvarez' derivation operator method for computing some brackets, and methods developed with Negron for computing brackets on resolutions that are differential graded coalgebras. We will introduce A-infinity coalgebras as a larger framework for some of these recent results, and make a connection to Stasheff's realization of the bracket as a graded commutator of coderivations on a tensor coalgebra.



Invited talks:

Joseph Chuang (City University)
Title: Derived rank functions on algebras

Abstract: Cohn and Schofield showed that (equivalence classes of) morphisms from a fixed algebra A to skew fields are parametrized by functions on finitely presented A-modules satisfying certain natural conditions, called rank functions. In this talk I will introduce a derived version of this theory, relating morphisms from A to dg skew fields in the homotopy category of dgas to functions on the derived category of A. This is joint work with Andrey Lazarev.


Karin Erdmann (Oxford)
Title: Periodicity: generalized quaternion type

Abstract: Let A be a tame symmetric algebra, which is indecomposable and of infinite representation type. We say that A is an algebra of generalized quaternion type (AGQT) if all simple A-modules have Ω-period four. Examples are blocks of finite groups with quaternion defect groups.

In this talk, we will discuss more generally periodicity for selfinjective algebras. We describe the classification of AGQT's whose Gabriel quiver is 2-regular (that is, two arrows end and two arrows start at each vertex). We also have constructed new AGQT's whose Gabriel quiver is not 2-regular, in a more general setting of weighted surface algebras. In addition, there are two exotic families. All AGQT's we found are shown to be periodic as algebras, of period four.

This is joint work with Andrzej Skowroński.


Estanislao Herscovich (Grenoble)
Title: Cyclic A-infinity-algebras and cyclic homology

Abstract: The aim of the talk is to provide a new description of the complex computing the Hochschild homology of an H-unitary A-infinity-algebra A as a derived tensor product satisfying that:
(1) there is a canonical morphism from it to the complex computing the cyclic homology of A that was introduced by M. Kontsevich and Y. Soibelman;
(2) this morphism induces the map I in the SBI sequence;
(3) its cohomology is canonically isomorphic to the space of morphisms from A to its dual in the derived category of A-infinity-bimodules.

As direct consequences I obtain the previous results proved by C.-H. Cho and S. Lee, as well as the fact that Koszul duality establishes a bijection between the almost exact d-Calabi-Yau property and the existence of strong homotopy inner products, extending a result proved by M. Van den Bergh.


Dmitri Kaledin (Steklov Institute / NRU HSE)
Title: Bokstedt periodicity and Bott periodicity

Abstract: Topological Hochschild Homology, while originally defined by topologists, has recently started turning up in many purely algebraic situations. This is especially true for algebras over a finite field Fp, due to a sort of a miracle: THH(Fp) is extremely simple, it is just the algebra of polynomials in one variable of degree 2. However, the existing proofs of this fact are not simple at all. I will present yet another proof that uses quite a few additional general structures THH is known to have (to wit, multiplication and a trace functor structure), but almost nothing specific to Fp.


Steffen Koenig (Stuttgart)
Title: Homotopy and homology of standardly filtered categories I

Abstract: Modules with standard filtrations occur for instance in algebraic Lie theory and in algebraic geometry, and generally in highest weight categories. They form exact, but usually not abelian subcategories of module categories of quasi-hereditary algebras. In joint work with Julian Küshammer and Sergiy Ovsienko, these exact categories have been described in terms of A-infinity structures (following work by Bernhard Keller) and as representations of bocses.

This talk will focus on the prehistory and the first part of this development and on applications, including a Poincaré-Birkhoff theorem for quasi-hereditary algebras and an interpretation of Ringel duality as Burt-Butler duality.


Julian Külshammer (Uppsala)
Title: Homotopy and homology of standardly filtered categories II

Abstract: Ringel duality is an important concept for quasi-hereditary algebras that exchanges modules with a standard filtration and modules with a costandard filtration for a different algebra. Using the description of quasi-hereditary algebras obtained in joint work with Steffen Koenig and Sergiy Ovsienko we will describe how to interpret Ringel duality as an instance of Koszul duality for differential graded algebras. This is joint work with Agnieszka Bodzenta based on ideas by Sergiy Ovsienko. Furthermore we will present a uniqueness result of bocses associated to quasi-hereditary algebras which is joint work in progress with Vanessa Miemietz.

Maria Julia Redondo (Universidad del Sur)
Title: Deformations of monomial algebras

Abstract: It is well known that deformation problems can be described in terms of differential graded Lie algebras, and that if F is a quasi-isomorphism of differential graded Lie algebras then F induces an equivalence between the corresponding deformation problems.
The problem of deforming the multiplication of a monomial algebra A can be described by the graded Lie algebra C(A,A)[1] which is the shifted Hochschild complex endowed with the Gerstenhaber bracket. However, Bardzell's complex B(A) has shown to be more efficient to compute Hochschild cohomology of monomial algebras. We use explict comparison morphisms between C(A,A) and B(A) in order to describe deformations of A by computing the corresponding Maurer-Cartan equation. In the particular case of infinitesimal deformations, we describe the quiver and relations of the deformed algebra in terms of the quiver and relations of A.


Sibylle Schroll (Leicester)
Title: On the Lie algebra structure of the first Hochschild cohomology

Abstract: The first Hochschild cohomology of a finite dimensional algebra equipped with the Gerstenhaber bracket is a Lie algebra. It is an interesting question to ask, which Lie algebras can actually appear in this way. In this talk we will analyse the structure of the first Hochschild cohomology as a Lie algebra and give some criteria for when it is solvable. As an application, we will show that for large classes of tame algebras the first Hochschild cohomology is solvable as a Lie algebra. This is joint work with Lleonard Rubio y Degrassi and Andrea Solotar.

Chelsea Walton (Urbana-Champaign)
Title: Tensor algebras in finite tensor categories

Abstract: I will discuss a method for classifying actions of finite-dimensional Hopf algebras on path algebras of quivers, and more generally on tensor algebras TB(V) where B is a semisimple k-algebra and V is a B-bimodule. This is done by working within the broader framework of finite (multi-)tensor categories C, parameterizing tensor algebras in C in terms of C-module categories. Examples will be discussed, especially for tensor algebras in group-theoretical fusion categories. This is joint work with Pavel Etingof and Ryan Kinser.


Contributed talks:

Marco Armenta (CIMAT)
Title: Derived invariance of operations in Hochschild theory

Abstract: We prove that a derived equivalence between two finite dimensional algebras induces isomorphisms between Hochschild homology and Hochschild cohomology that respects simultaneously the cup product, the cap product, the Gerstenhaber bracket and the Connes differential. We do this with the use of Morita theory for derived categories and the cyclic functor defined by Keller to prove derived invariance of cyclic homology.


Cristian Chaparro (Buenos Aires)
Title: The first Hochschild cohomology group of Brauer graph algebras with multiplicity one

Abstract: Brauer graph algebras with multiplicity one can be seen like the trivial extension of gentle algebras and the first Hochschild cohomology of the trivial extension of a finite dimensional algebra can be computed as a direct sum of four vector spaces. We compute the four vector spaces for the trivial extension of gentle algebras and give an explicit description of the Lie algebra structure of the first Hochschild cohomology group. This is a joint work with Sibylle Schroll and Andrea Solotar.


Claude Cibils (Montpellier)
Title: Han's conjecture for split extensions

An algebra A is a split extension if A=B⊕ M, where B is a subalgebra and M is a two-sided ideal of A. For instance, if N is a B-bimodule, the tensor algebra T=TB(N) is a split extension, as well as T/J for J a two-sided ideal of T contained in its positive part.
Let A be a finite dimensional algebra over a field. Han's conjecture states that if the Hochschild homology of A vanishes in high degrees, then A is smooth, that is A is of finite global dimension. Let H be the class of finite dimensional algebras which verify Han's conjecture.
In this talk we will consider sufficient conditions in order that H is closed under split extensions. This is a joint work with M. Lanzilotta, E. Marcos and A. Solotar.


Lleonard Rubio Y Degrassi (University of Murcia)
Title: On the simplicity of the first Hochschild cohomology

Abstract: In this talk I will show how the simplicity, as a Lie algebra, of the first Hochschild cohomology is strongly related with the number of vertices and arrows in the Ext-quiver of a finite dimensional algebra A. In the first part I will prove that for a block B of group algebra kG of a finite group G with one simple module, HH1(B) is a simple Lie algebra if and only if B is nilpotent (Morita equivalent to the group algebra of its defect group) with an elementary abelian defect group P of order at least 3. In the second part I will show that for an arbitrary finite dimensional algebra A, if we allow up to two parallel arrows in the same direction in the Ext-quiver of A but no loops, then the only simple Lie algebra that could arise in this case is sl(2,k), with char(k)≠ 2. This is joint work with Markus Linckelmann.


Pedro Tamaroff (Trinity College Dublin)
Title: Minimal models for monomial algebras

Abstract: In 1985, David Anick came up with a combinatorial construction of chains which can be used to compute various homological invariants of an associative algebra from a confluent presentation of such an algebra by generators and relations. In particular, for algebras with monomial relations, his construction produces those invariants directly.

In this talk, I will explain how to compute a rich algebraic structure on Anick chains leading to an explicit formula for the minimal model of a monomial algebra. This computation relies on algebraic discrete Morse theory and on homotopy transfer formulas; those are formulas perfectly suited for homological computations where underlying chain complexes are of combinatorial nature.


Yury Volkov (Saint Petersburg)
Title: Graded center and generalized Reynolds ideals

Abstract: We will discuss the notion of the graded center of a category and introduce the dual notion of the graded abelianization. Then we define the so-called generalized Reynolds ideals in the graded center. We show that the generalized Reynolds ideals in the graded center of the homotopy category of perfect complexes induce ideals in the Hochschild cohomology of the algebra under consideration. Finally, we develop relations between the obtained ideals in the Hochschild cohomology and generalized Reynolds ideals in the center of the algebra. This talk is based on a joint work with Alexandra Zvonareva.
